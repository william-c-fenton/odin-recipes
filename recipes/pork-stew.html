Pork Stew

Description:

This is a classic tomato, vegetable, and pork stew perfect for a fall day. Or any day, really.

This recipe has a long cooking time, but otherwise is not very difficult. Perfect for tending in the background while you do other things.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp chopped parsley
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1.5 tbsp seasoned salt
  • 1.5 tbsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp dried marjoram
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 3 lbs boneless pork shoulder, cubed
  • 1 bunch spinach leaves, washed
  • 0.25 cups olive oil
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 0.25 cups butter
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 1 lbs fresh mushrooms, sliced
  • 1.5 cups beef broth
  • 2 cans stewed tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 cans butter beans
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toss onion powder, parsley, 1 tbsp garlic powder, seasoned salt, 1.5 tbsp black pepper, marjoram, cinnamon, and nutmeg in a large plastic bag. Add the cubed pork, toss until coated, then seal and refrigerate overnight.
  2. Preheat oven to 250 degrees, line a 9x9 in baking dish with half of the spinach
  3. Pack the marinated pork into the dish and cover with the remaining spinach leaves. Cover the pan with foil and bake for 3 hours, or until tender.
  4. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Whisk in the flour, and cook for 15 minutes until the flour has toasted. Scrape into a dish and set aside.
  5. Melt the butter in the pot, and stir in the onions and celery. Cook and stir until the onion has softened and turned translucent, about 10 minutes. Add the mushrooms and cook until tender. Stir the flour back into the pot to coat the vegetables.
  6. Pour in the broth, tomatoes, cooked pork and spinach, bay leaves, red pepper flakes, and 1tsp garlic powder. Simmer for 1.5 hours.
  7. Stir in the butter beans, squash, and zucchini. Simmer 30 minutes until the vegetables are tender. Season to taste with salt and pepper before serving.
